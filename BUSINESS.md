## üìà Business Overview

### Sip History - Interactive Cocktail Classes

**Concept:**
- 2-hour interactive cocktail classes in off-hours bars
- 20-25% structured, 80% improvised
- Mix of history, technique, hands-on, jokes, drinking
- Three cocktails included (chronological history: daiquiri ‚Üí old-fashioned ‚Üí bee's knees)
- Host to guest ratio: 1:20 (sometimes 2:40 on busy nights)

**Business Model (Win-Win-Win-Win):**
1. **Guests win:** Unique experience, fun, educational, 5000+ 5-star reviews
2. **Bars win:** Brings people during dead hours, 10% of ticket sales for doing nothing
3. **Hosts win:** Pre-shifted, put on a show, great tips, same money as 6-8 hour shift in 3-4 hours
4. **Owners win:** Insanely high profit margin (~60% even after paying hosts)

**Key Methodology: "Zoom In and Zoom Out"**

Two meanings:
1. **Read the room:** Handle mixed groups (bachelorette parties + serious cocktail nerds)
   - Example: 18 seats bachelorette, 2 seats couple ‚Äî blast with the party, nerd out with the couple
   - Make it fun for everyone no matter who shows up
   
2. **Ebb and flow:** Balance history, technique, drinking, fun, jokes
   - Know when to interrupt flow for history (short, snappy, concise)
   - Script evolved over time (now pretty much perfect)
   - Stagger attention, keep it dynamic

### Alchemix - Retail Products

**Product Line:**

## üéØ Key Business Concepts

### Location Escape Velocity

**Definition:** The ability to launch a new location, get it busy enough to support a host full or part-time, and then leave to scale the next location.

**Critical bottleneck:** As bootstrapped startup, Tommy has to:
1. Go to new location
2. Host classes himself initially (business sustains him)
3. Find the right person
4. Get location busy enough for host to make good living
5. Hand off fully and move to next location

**Challenges:**
- Hosts need to be on-call as location gets busy
- Hosts need other job to sustain themselves initially
- Delicate balance: host needs enough classes to go full/part-time before Tommy can leave
- If host quits when location is busy = Tommy has to fly in immediately (doesn't scale)

**Solution in progress:**
- Finding hosts faster
- Getting locations busy faster (ads, marketing, review strategy)
- Professional training program (feeder system for talent)

### Getting a New Location Busy Fast

**Current problem:** Bookings always start slow.

**Why slow:**
- Need reviews across all platforms (Viator, GetYourGuide, Airbnb, Google)
- OTAs don't promote you until you have reviews
- Takes months to build up

**Solution strategy:**
1. Run Facebook + Google ads to collect direct bookings (needs refinement)
2. Plug-and-play launch process
3. Rapid review generation
4. Get to 50-100 reviews per platform ASAP

**Goal:** Launch location ‚Üí busy in weeks, not months ‚Üí hire host ‚Üí move on.

### New Orleans Bar Disaster (April-Aug 2025)

**What happened:**
- Met at Tales of the Cocktail 2024
- Real estate agent connected them with "worst human ever"
- Management services agreement for struggling bar (Duplex)
- Owner had juice bar downstairs, bar upstairs
- Didn't find out till later: Owner owed landlord $100k
- Landlord evicted owner within 5 months
- Owner ransacked and wrecked the place
- New Orleans laws made it hard to fight
- Low capital lost (didn't invest in build-out), but lots of time lost

**Lessons:**
- Good business deal with wrong person = disaster
- Someone willing to commit crimes can fuck you over
- Vet partners thoroughly
- Don't get distracted by shiny opportunities (opening a bar)
- Cocktail class model is more scalable than bar ownership

### Platform Learning Curve

**Mistakes:**
- Waited too long to add Viator (Feb 2023, should have been earlier)
- Did a lot wrong with platforms initially
- Learned too late: need independent listing on each platform
- Hard to stand out from noise

**Lessons:**
- OTAs are critical for tourism business
- Reviews across all platforms = SEO for discovery
- Platform strategy from day 1 in new markets
